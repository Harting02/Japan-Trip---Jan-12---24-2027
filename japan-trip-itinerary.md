# Japan Trip — Jan 13–24, 2027

**Flights:** Kuching (KCH) → Narita (NRT) via Bandar Seri Begawan (BI-856/BI-695), arrive Narita 07:30, Jan 13
Return: Narita → Kuching via BSB (BI-696/BI-855), depart Narita 11:45, Jan 24

**Group:** 3–5 people
**Vibe:** Mix of relaxed sightseeing + a couple of active/snow days
**Route:** Tokyo → Hakone → Nagano → Kyoto → Nara (day trip) → Osaka → back to Tokyo for the flight

---

## Itinerary

### Tokyo (3 nights: Jan 13–15)

**Jan 13 — Arrival**
- Land Narita 07:30, transfer into the city, check in, rest
- Evening: Senso-ji, Asakusa (go after dark, lit up and quieter)
- **Transport:** Narita → Tokyo. Look out for the Keisei **Skyliner** (to Nippori/Ueno) at ¥2,310–2,570 (~RM58–65), 41 min — cheapest and fastest; or JR **Narita Express (N'EX)** direct to Shinjuku/Shibuya at ¥3,070 one-way (~RM77; ~¥5,000–5,200 / ~RM126–131 round-trip), 75–90 min. N'EX round-trip fare is covered if activating a JR Pass that day; Skyliner is private (Keisei) and not JR Pass-eligible.

**Jan 14 — Market & Shibuya**
- Breakfast at Tsukiji Outer Market (before 9am for the best stalls)
- Afternoon: Ginza / teamLab (optional)
- Evening: Shibuya Crossing + Shibuya Sky at dusk

**Jan 15 — Local Tokyo**
- Morning: Meiji Shrine / Harajuku
- Afternoon: Yanaka Ginza — old-school neighborhood, cash-only local shops, good contrast to Shibuya
- Dinner: Omoide Yokocho, Shinjuku (tiny yakitori alleys)

### Hakone (1 night: Jan 16)
- Morning train out via Odakyu Romancecar
- Owakudani — volcanic valley walk, Fuji views (weather permitting), try the black eggs
- Afternoon: Lake Ashi pirate-ship cruise
- Overnight: onsen ryokan, kaiseki dinner + hot spring soak
- **Transport:** Tokyo → Hakone. Look out for the Odakyu **Romancecar** (Shinjuku → Hakone-Yumoto), ¥2,420–2,470 one-way (~RM61–62), ~80–90 min. Better value: the **Hakone Freepass** (¥7,100 / ~RM178, 2-day) covers local buses, the Owakudani ropeway, and the Lake Ashi cruise — worth it given the day's plans — plus the Romancecar seat surcharge (~¥2,400 / ~RM60 round-trip).

### Nagano / Shibu Onsen (1 night: Jan 17–18)
**Jan 17**
- Short lakeside/shrine hike near Hakone in the morning
- Train to Nagano / Yudanaka
- Evening: stroll through Shibu Onsen — historic hot spring town, 9 public bathhouses
- **Transport:** Hakone → Yudanaka. Chain: Odakyu bus/train Hakone-Yumoto → Odawara (~¥1,520 / ~RM38) → Tokaido Shinkansen Odawara → Tokyo/Omiya → Hokuriku Shinkansen "**Kagayaki**" to Nagano (~¥8,000–8,340 / ~RM201–209) → Nagaden Line ltd. express "**Snow Monkey/Yukemuri**" to Yudanaka (¥1,290 / ~RM32). ~3.5–4 hrs total. The Nagaden Line is private and **not** covered by a JR Pass, even though the Hokuriku Shinkansen leg is.

**Jan 18**
- Early morning: hike into Jigokudani Snow Monkey Park (~25 min through snowy forest, wild monkeys in hot springs)
- Train to Kyoto via Nagoya (scenic Kiso Valley route)
- **Transport:** Yudanaka → Kyoto. Nagaden back to Nagano → ltd. express "**Shinano**" to Nagoya (this genuinely runs through the Kiso Valley — Kiso-Fukushima, Nagiso stops — so the scenic label checks out): ¥6,930 non-reserved (~RM174) / ¥7,660 reserved (~RM192), ~3 hrs → Tokaido Shinkansen Nagoya → Kyoto (~¥5,700 / ~RM143, ~35 min).

### Kyoto (3 nights: Jan 19–21)

**Jan 19 — Shrines & temples**
- Early morning: Fushimi Inari Taisha (before 8am to beat crowds)
- Afternoon: Kiyomizu-dera + Higashiyama old streets
- Evening: Gion

**Jan 20 — Nara day trip + market**
- Day trip to Nara: Todai-ji, Great Buddha Hall, free-roaming deer park (~45 min by train)
- Evening: dinner at Nishiki Market (bring cash)
- **Transport:** Kyoto ↔ Nara. JR **Nara Line Miyakoji Rapid** — ¥720 each way (~RM18), 45 min, JR Pass-eligible; or Kintetsu Nara Line Express — ¥760–1,280 each way (~RM19–32), 40–55 min, station more central for Todai-ji/deer park. Take JR if holding a pass, Kintetsu if paying cash and want the shorter walk.

**Jan 21 — Bamboo grove, then Osaka**
- Morning: Arashiyama Bamboo Forest (early = quieter) + Tenryu-ji
- Afternoon: train to Osaka
- Evening: Dotonbori — neon signs, street food
- **Transport:** Kyoto → Osaka. JR **Special Rapid** — ¥580 (~RM15), 28 min; or Hankyu Ltd Express — ¥410 (~RM10), 42 min, scenic route through Kawaramachi (not JR Pass-eligible but cheaper).

### Osaka (2 nights: Jan 22–23)

**Jan 22 — Market & castle**
- Breakfast at Kuromon Market (before 1pm, popular stalls sell out)
- Osaka Castle grounds + observatory
- Free time: Shinsaibashi shopping

**Jan 23 — Travel day**
- Free morning
- Afternoon: shinkansen back toward Tokyo/Narita, overnight near the airport
- **Transport:** Osaka → Narita area. Shinkansen "**Nozomi**" Shin-Osaka → Tokyo — ¥13,870 (~RM348), fastest at 2.5–3 hrs (not JR Pass-eligible; take "Hikari" instead if using a pass, +30 min) → N'EX Tokyo → Narita, ¥3,070 (~RM77), ~1 hr. ~3h40 door to door.

**Jan 24 — Departure**
- Early transfer to Narita, flight 11:45

## Logistics Notes

- **Transport cost estimate:** ~¥45,000–50,000 (~RM1,130–1,255) per person for the whole trip (excl. flights), given the mixed JR/private-line route (Odakyu, Nagaden, Kintetsu, Hankyu all appear and aren't JR Pass-eligible).
- **JR Pass:** Skip the classic 7-day nationwide pass for this route — since its last price hike it doesn't pencil out, and several legs (Odakyu Romancecar, Nagaden to Yudanaka, Kintetsu to Nara, Hankyu to Osaka) aren't covered anyway. Buy point-to-point instead, and get the **Hakone Freepass** (¥7,100 / ~RM178, 2-day) locally for the Hakone day — it covers the buses, ropeway, and Lake Ashi cruise.
- **Exchange rate used:** ¥1 ≈ RM 0.0251 (Jul 2026) — MYR figures above are approximate, check the live rate closer to booking.
- **Luggage forwarding (takkyubin):** With 5 city changes, forward big suitcases hotel-to-hotel and carry only a day bag on travel days.
- **Language:** Major stations, menus, and signage have enough English; Google Translate's camera mode covers the rest.
- **January cold:** Layers needed. Kyoto/Osaka ~5–10°C daytime; Nagano/Hakone colder with snow. Grippy shoes for the Jigokudani trail.
- **Book ahead:** Hakone ryokan, Shibu Onsen ryokan, and any teamLab tickets — January is peak onsen season, book a few months out.
