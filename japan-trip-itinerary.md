# Japan Trip — Jan 13–24, 2027

**Flights:** Kuching (KCH) → Narita (NRT) via Bandar Seri Begawan (BI-856/BI-695), arrive Narita 07:30, Jan 13
Return: Narita → Kuching via BSB (BI-696/BI-855), depart Narita 11:45, Jan 24

**Group:** 3–5 people
**Vibe:** Mix of relaxed sightseeing + a couple of active/snow days
**Route:** Tokyo → Hakone → Nagano → Kyoto → Nara (day trip) → Osaka → back to Tokyo for the flight

---

## Itinerary

### Tokyo (3 nights: Jan 13–15)

**Jan 13 — Arrival**
- Land Narita 07:30, transfer into the city, check in, rest
- Evening: Senso-ji, Asakusa (go after dark, lit up and quieter)

**Jan 14 — Market & Shibuya**
- Breakfast at Tsukiji Outer Market (before 9am for the best stalls)
- Afternoon: Ginza / teamLab (optional)
- Evening: Shibuya Crossing + Shibuya Sky at dusk

**Jan 15 — Local Tokyo**
- Morning: Meiji Shrine / Harajuku
- Afternoon: Yanaka Ginza — old-school neighborhood, cash-only local shops, good contrast to Shibuya
- Dinner: Omoide Yokocho, Shinjuku (tiny yakitori alleys)

### Hakone (1 night: Jan 16)
- Morning train out via Odakyu Romancecar
- Owakudani — volcanic valley walk, Fuji views (weather permitting), try the black eggs
- Afternoon: Lake Ashi pirate-ship cruise
- Overnight: onsen ryokan, kaiseki dinner + hot spring soak

### Nagano / Shibu Onsen (1 night: Jan 17–18)
**Jan 17**
- Short lakeside/shrine hike near Hakone in the morning
- Train to Nagano / Yudanaka
- Evening: stroll through Shibu Onsen — historic hot spring town, 9 public bathhouses

**Jan 18**
- Early morning: hike into Jigokudani Snow Monkey Park (~25 min through snowy forest, wild monkeys in hot springs)
- Train to Kyoto via Nagoya (scenic Kiso Valley route)

### Kyoto (3 nights: Jan 19–21)

**Jan 19 — Shrines & temples**
- Early morning: Fushimi Inari Taisha (before 8am to beat crowds)
- Afternoon: Kiyomizu-dera + Higashiyama old streets
- Evening: Gion

**Jan 20 — Nara day trip + market**
- Day trip to Nara: Todai-ji, Great Buddha Hall, free-roaming deer park (~45 min by train)
- Evening: dinner at Nishiki Market (bring cash)

**Jan 21 — Bamboo grove, then Osaka**
- Morning: Arashiyama Bamboo Forest (early = quieter) + Tenryu-ji
- Afternoon: train to Osaka
- Evening: Dotonbori — neon signs, street food

### Osaka (2 nights: Jan 22–23)

**Jan 22 — Market & castle**
- Breakfast at Kuromon Market (before 1pm, popular stalls sell out)
- Osaka Castle grounds + observatory
- Free time: Shinsaibashi shopping

**Jan 23 — Travel day**
- Free morning
- Afternoon: shinkansen back toward Tokyo/Narita, overnight near the airport

**Jan 24 — Departure**
- Early transfer to Narita, flight 11:45

## Logistics Notes

- **JR Pass:** With this many long-distance legs (Hakone, Nagano, Kyoto, Nara, Osaka), price out a 7- or 14-day JR Pass before buying individual tickets.
- **Luggage forwarding (takkyubin):** With 5 city changes, forward big suitcases hotel-to-hotel and carry only a day bag on travel days.
- **Language:** Major stations, menus, and signage have enough English; Google Translate's camera mode covers the rest.
- **January cold:** Layers needed. Kyoto/Osaka ~5–10°C daytime; Nagano/Hakone colder with snow. Grippy shoes for the Jigokudani trail.
- **Book ahead:** Hakone ryokan, Shibu Onsen ryokan, and any teamLab tickets — January is peak onsen season, book a few months out.
